# Struggled to Adjust
### A fictional psychological drama about isolation, choices, and healing.

## Short Description
This repository contains the website for *Struggled to Adjust*, a fictionalized story inspired by the author’s real-life emotions and experiences.
It explores mental health, empathy, social isolation, and personal growth through a dark but reflective narrative.

## Story Summary
Struggled to Adjust follows Bobby, a high school student navigating isolation, frustration, and the consequences of unchecked anger.
In Grade 9 at Dalisay High School (2017–2018), Bobby is separated from his close friends (“katropa”) when he is placed in Section 9-Golf while his friends are assigned to 9-India. Struggling to adjust, he becomes withdrawn and increasingly irritable. Minor incidents—including a confrontation with Clarisse Mei Tan—trigger his anger, causing him to lash out at classmates.
Despite the efforts of Lawrence Allan Solinap, the only student who tries to befriend him, Bobby’s mental state worsens. Hallucinations and paranoia escalate, leading to violent actions: he coerces Amelia Nicole Santos into apologizing and ultimately fatally stabs her.
Bobby is expelled and sent to a juvenile detention center. Three years later, he is tried for murder, found guilty, and sentenced to life imprisonment. Meanwhile, Amelia’s family, friends, and classmates work to honor her memory, finding ways to cope and remember her as a kind and caring individual.
The story explores themes of isolation, anger, mental health, empathy, and personal responsibility. It serves as a cautionary tale about the dangers of unchecked emotions and social disconnection, while highlighting the importance of communication, understanding, and emotional resilience.
